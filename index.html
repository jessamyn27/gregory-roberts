Gregory Roberts is an accomplished musician and songwriter, most notably as founding member of the critically-acclaimed NYC band White Rabbits. After three successful albums, a handful of late-night TV performances and countless world tours, Gregory left New York for the greener pastures of Austin, Texas. It was here he expanded his repertoire to include commercial composition, music supervision, creative direction and sonic brand identity for clients such as Tommy John, The Black Tux, Casper Mattress and Saint Archer Brewery. Gregory also branched out into experiential marketing, working on interactive installations for brands like Adidas, Equinox and UberEats at music festivals like Something In The Water and FORM.


After establishing himself as an in-demand creative collaborator, Gregory’s next move was to embrace the entrepreneurial spirit of Austin, and so he pivoted towards the city’s burgeoning tech industry where he joined up with the Hult Prize-winning startup Aspire as their operations manager. Approaching this new role with his same creative spirit Gregory helped build the first fully-automated cricket farm in North America aimed at solving the world’s growing food insecurities crisis.


With one foot now firmly planted in this new world of tech and the other still in music, Gregory combined skillsets to become a senior music publishing researcher for Meta’s content operations team, helping to build a complete and comprehensive digital rights database for the world’s largest social media platform. During the pandemic he proved himself to be an integral asset and was promoted to the music research training lead where he not only created all of the relevant content and learning materials for 8 ad-hoc workflows, but lead all training sessions for the 60-plus member team.


Most recently Gregory has returned to freelance collaboration, this time working on a similar digital publishing rights project for the social media giant TikTok. He lives on Austin’s shady, tree-lined Eastside with his wife Jessamyn, their two dogs Max and Popeye and their cat Pina.
